## 5. DUE DILIGENCE PROCESS

### Phase 1: Initial Diligence (Days 1-7)

**Documents to Request:**
1. **Rent Roll:** Last 12 months + current
2. **Operating Statements:** Last 3 years of P&Ls
3. **Tax Returns:** Schedule E for last 2 years
4. **Leases:** All current tenant leases
5. **Property Taxes:** Last 2 years tax bills
6. **Insurance:** Current policy and claims history
7. **Utilities:** Last 12 months of bills
8. **Violations:** Department of Buildings report
9. **Title Report:** Preliminary title commitment
10. **Surveys:** ALTA survey if available

**Key Diligence Items:**
- ‚úÖ **Rent Roll Verification:** Call tenants to verify occupancy, rent amounts
- ‚úÖ **Physical Inspection:** Walk every unit, common areas, mechanicals
- ‚úÖ **Title Review:** Check for liens, encumbrances, easements
- ‚úÖ **Zoning Compliance:** Verify building is legal, certificates of occupancy
- ‚úÖ **Building Violations:** DOB, HPD violations that must be cured
- ‚úÖ **Environmental:** Phase I ESA for commercial properties

### Phase 2: Deep Diligence (Days 8-30)

**Property Inspection:**
1. **Structural:** Foundation, roof, exterior walls
2. **Mechanical:** Boiler, HVAC, plumbing, electrical
3. **Units:** Kitchens, baths, flooring, windows
4. **Common Areas:** Hallways, stairs, lobby
5. **Life Safety:** Fire alarm, sprinklers, exits
6. **Capital Items:** Roof age, boiler age, elevator (if applicable)

**Financial Diligence:**
1. **Rent Comps:** Validate market rents with 5+ recent comps
2. **Expense Comps:** Benchmark to similar buildings
3. **Vacancy Trends:** Analyze unit turnover and vacancy days
4. **Rent Increases:** Check legal limits (NYC rent stabilization)
5. **Property Tax Appeal:** Research assessment and comparable taxes

**Legal Diligence:**
1. **Tenant Files:** Review all tenant applications, background checks
2. **Lease Compliance:** Ensure leases comply with local law
3. **Eviction History:** Check housing court records
4. **Zoning Conformity:** Confirm legal units and use
5. **Entity Structure:** Review ownership structure and transfer restrictions

### Phase 3: Closing Preparation (Days 31-45)

**Loan Documentation:**
- **Appraisal:** Lender-ordered appraisal
- **Loan Commitment:** Signed loan commitment letter
- **Title Insurance:** Final title policy
- **Survey:** Updated ALTA survey
- **Insurance:** Property insurance binder

**Purchase Agreement Terms:**
- **Earnest Money:** Typically 5-10% of purchase price
- **Contingencies:** Inspection, financing, title
- **Closing Date:** 45-60 days from contract signing
- **Seller Representations:** Rent roll, violations, litigation
- **Post-Closing Adjustments:** Prorations, security deposits

### Financing Options

**1. Conventional Bank Loan**
- **LTV:** 65-75%
- **Rate:** 6.5-7.5% (floating or fixed)
- **Amortization:** 25-30 years
- **Term:** 5-10 years
- **Recourse:** Often full recourse for small properties
- **Best For:** Strong borrower, stabilized property

**2. Agency Debt (Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac)**
- **LTV:** Up to 80% (for 5+ units)
- **Rate:** 5.5-6.5% (typically better than banks)
- **Amortization:** 30 years
- **Term:** 7, 10, 12 years
- **Recourse:** Non-recourse (with carve-outs)
- **Best For:** 5+ units, stabilized, strong location
- **Min Property Value:** Often $1M+

**3. HUD/FHA Multifamily Loans**
- **LTV:** Up to 85-90%
- **Rate:** Very competitive (5.0-6.0%)
- **Amortization:** 35-40 years
- **Term:** 35-40 years (fully amortizing)
- **Recourse:** Non-recourse
- **Best For:** Value-add, affordable housing
- **Drawback:** Complex underwriting, 6-12 month process

**4. Private/Hard Money**
- **LTV:** 60-75%
- **Rate:** 9-12% (higher risk)
- **Amortization:** Interest-only or 15-20 years
- **Term:** 1-3 years (bridge)
- **Recourse:** Often full recourse
- **Best For:** Quick close, heavy value-add, distressed

**5. Portfolio Loan**
- **LTV:** 65-80%
- **Rate:** 6.0-7.5%
- **Amortization:** 25-30 years
- **Term:** 5-7 years
- **Cross-Collateralized:** Multiple properties
- **Best For:** Acquiring multiple small buildings

## 8. RISK ASSESSMENT

### Key Risks in Small Multifamily

**1. Market Risk**
- **Rental Market Weakness:** Recession, job losses, oversupply
- **Mitigation:** Buy in strong, diverse economy markets
- **Likelihood:** Medium | **Impact:** High

**2. Financing Risk**
- **Interest Rate Increases:** Floating rate debt, refinance risk
- **Mitigation:** Lock long-term fixed rates, stress test at +200 bps
- **Likelihood:** High | **Impact:** Medium

**3. Operating Risk**
- **Unexpected Expenses:** Major repairs, deferred maintenance
- **Mitigation:** Thorough inspection, adequate reserves
- **Likelihood:** Medium | **Impact:** Medium

**4. Tenant Risk**
- **High Vacancy:** Economic downturn, tenant turnover
- **Mitigation:** Underwrite conservative 5-7% vacancy
- **Likelihood:** Low (in strong markets) | **Impact:** High

**5. Regulatory Risk**
- **Rent Control:** NYC, California, Oregon, other cities
- **Mitigation:** Understand local laws, buy free-market units
- **Likelihood:** Medium (location-dependent) | **Impact:** Very High

**6. Execution Risk**
- **Renovation Delays:** Contractor issues, cost overruns
- **Mitigation:** Detailed scope, fixed-price contracts, contingency
- **Likelihood:** High | **Impact:** Medium

**7. Exit Risk**
- **Lack of Buyers:** Illiquid market, limited buyers for small buildings
- **Mitigation:** Buy well, create value, have long hold horizon
- **Likelihood:** Low (in good markets) | **Impact:** Medium

### Risk Mitigation Strategies

**1. Conservative Underwriting**
- Vacancy: 5-7% (vs. 3% actual)
- Expense Growth: 3-4% (vs. 2.5% typical)
- Rent Growth: 2-3% (vs. 3-5% potential)
- Cap Rate Compression: 0 bps (don't assume)
- CapEx Reserve: $500/unit (minimum)

**2. Financial Cushion**
- Hold 10% of equity as operating reserve
- Maintain 1.30x+ DSCR (debt service coverage)
- Stress test at +200 bps higher rates
- Model worst-case scenarios (recession, 15% vacancy)

**3. Strong Tenant Selection**
- Credit score minimum: 650+
- Income requirement: 40x monthly rent
- Background check: Criminal, eviction history
- Employment verification
- Reference checks

## üìö APPENDIX

### A. Glossary of Key Terms

**Cap Rate (Capitalization Rate):** NOI / Purchase Price. Measures return on investment without financing.

**Cash-on-Cash Return:** Annual pre-tax cash flow / Total equity invested. Measures cash return to equity.

**DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio):** NOI / Debt Service. Measures ability to cover debt payments.

**EGI (Effective Gross Income):** Gross potential rent - Vacancy + Other income.

**IRR (Internal Rate of Return):** Discount rate where NPV = 0. Measures time-value-adjusted return.

**LTV (Loan-to-Value):** Loan Amount / Property Value. Measures leverage.

**Mark-to-Market:** Difference between in-place rent and current market rent. Measures rent upside.

**NOI (Net Operating Income):** EGI - Operating Expenses. Measures property profitability.

**Pro Forma:** Forward-looking financial projection based on assumptions, not historical actuals.

**T-12 (Trailing 12 Months):** Actual historical financials for the past 12 months.

**Value-Add:** Investment strategy focused on improving property to increase NOI and value.
