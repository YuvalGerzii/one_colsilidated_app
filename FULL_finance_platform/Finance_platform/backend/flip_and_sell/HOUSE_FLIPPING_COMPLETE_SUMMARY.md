### Key Metrics Calculated
1. **Total Cash Out:** All expenses across 6 months
2. **Total Cash In:** Sale proceeds
3. **Net Profit:** Total in - total out
4. **Maximum Capital Required:** Peak negative cash position
5. **Months to Break Even:** When cumulative becomes positive
6. **Annualized Return:** ROI adjusted for 6-month timeline

### Cost Breakdown by Phase
- **Acquisition:** Purchase price + closing costs
- **Renovation:** Total reno / 3 months + holding costs each month
- **Marketing:** Staging + photos + holding costs
- **Sale:** Commission (6%) + closing (1.5%) + staging

### Cumulative Tracking
The **Cumulative** column shows your running total:
- Starts deeply negative (after purchase)
- Gets more negative during renovation
- Becomes positive at sale
- Final number = your net profit

### How to Use
1. Timeline auto-populates from **Inputs** sheet
2. Renovation cost divides over 3 months
3. Holding costs calculate monthly based on Inputs
4. Sale happens at ARV from Inputs
5. Watch **Maximum Capital Required** = peak funding needed

### Pro Tips

- Peak negative cash = how much you need available

## ğŸ†• SHEET 6: FINANCING COMPARISON (NEW!)

### What It Does
**Side-by-side comparison** of 4 financing strategies

### The 4 Strategies Compared

#### Strategy 1: **ALL CASH**
**How it works:** Pay for everything with your own money

**Pros:**
- Highest ROI (no interest costs)
- Simple & clean exit
- No loan fees or points
- Fastest close

**Cons:**
- Ties up all your capital
- Limits deal flow
- Opportunity cost
- Can't scale easily

**Best for:**
- You have the cash available
- Want maximum profit per deal
- Low risk tolerance
- First-time flippers

#### Strategy 2: **HARD MONEY LOAN**
**How it works:** 75% LTC loan at 10% interest + 3% points

**Pros:**
- Quick approval (days not weeks)
- No income verification
- Based on property value
- Enables leverage

**Cons:**
- High interest rate (10%+)
- Points upfront (3% = $6k on $200k)
- Short-term only (6-12 months)
- Expensive if deal drags

**Best for:**
- Limited capital
- Need fast closing
- Credit issues
- Experienced flippers

**Example Costs (on $200k project):**
- Loan Amount: $150,000 (75% LTC)
- Points (3%): $4,500 upfront
- Interest (6 months at 10%): $7,500
- Total Cost: $12,000

#### Strategy 3: **PRIVATE MONEY**
**How it works:** 70% LTC loan from private investor at 8% interest

**Pros:**
- Lower rates than hard money
- Flexible terms
- Relationship-based
- No points typically

**Cons:**
- Requires network
- Takes time to arrange
- May be limited funds
- Personal liability

**Best for:**
- Have investor relationships
- Want lower cost capital
- Can wait for funding
- Building portfolio

**Example Costs (on $200k project):**
- Loan Amount: $140,000 (70% LTC)
- Points: $0 (usually)
- Interest (6 months at 8%): $5,600
- Total Cost: $5,600

#### Strategy 4: **BRRRR** (Buy, Rehab, Rent, Refinance, Repeat)
**How it works:** Refi at 75% ARV after renovation, keep as rental

**Pros:**
- Keep the property
- Pull out most capital
- Monthly cash flow
- Build rental portfolio
- Scale faster

**Cons:**
- Landlord responsibilities
- Slower capital recovery
- Must qualify for refinance
- Property management needed

**Best for:**
- Building rental portfolio
- Want monthly income
- Can handle tenants
- Long-term wealth focus

**Example:**
- ARV: $290,000
- Refinance at 75%: $217,500
- Original Investment: $200,000
- Cash Back: $17,500
- Plus: Monthly rent income
- Result: Recoup most money + keep property
