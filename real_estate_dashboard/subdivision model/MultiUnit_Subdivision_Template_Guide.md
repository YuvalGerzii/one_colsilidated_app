## üí° THE 5 EXIT STRATEGIES

### Strategy 1: SUBDIVIDE/CONVERT & SELL INDIVIDUALLY

**What It Is:**
Convert multi-unit property into separate legal ownership units (condos, townhomes, co-ops) and sell each unit individually to owner-occupants.

**Process:**
1. Legal conversion (3-12 months)
   - File conversion documents
   - Establish HOA/co-op
   - Separate utilities if needed
   - Create individual titles
2. Renovation (2-4 months per unit)
3. Marketing & sales (1-3 months per unit)

**Financial Profile:**
- **Profit Potential:** HIGHEST (20-40% premium vs as-is)
- **Timeline:** LONGEST (9-18 months)
- **Complexity:** HIGHEST
- **Risk:** MEDIUM (market timing, approval delays)

**Best For:**
- Strong condo/for-sale market
- Premium location
- Units in good condition
- You have 12+ months
- Maximum profit goal

**Typical ROI:** 500-1,500%+ (on down payment)

---

### Strategy 2: SELL AS-IS (MULTI-UNIT INVESTOR SALE)

**What It Is:**
Minimal improvements, sell as operating multi-unit property to another investor based on cap rate/income approach.

**Process:**
1. Light cosmetics (1-3 months)
2. Increase rents to market
3. Market to investors
4. Sell on cap rate basis

**Financial Profile:**
- **Profit Potential:** MEDIUM (standard market value)
- **Timeline:** SHORTEST (3-6 months)
- **Complexity:** LOWEST
- **Risk:** LOW (straightforward transaction)

**Best For:**
- Quick exit needed
- Simple transaction preferred
- Investor buyer pool available
- Conservative approach
- Limited capital/time

**Typical ROI:** 200-400% (on down payment)

---

### Strategy 3: BRRRR (Buy, Renovate, Rent, Refinance, Repeat)

**What It Is:**
Renovate all units, stabilize at market rents, refinance at 75% LTV, pull out capital, keep as rental portfolio.

**Process:**
1. Renovate units (3-6 months)
2. Increase rents to market
3. Stabilize occupancy (6-12 months)
4. Refinance (30 days)
5. Hold long-term or repeat

**Financial Profile:**
- **Profit Potential:** ONGOING (cash flow + appreciation)
- **Timeline:** LONG-TERM (12+ months to refi, then hold)
- **Complexity:** MEDIUM
- **Risk:** MEDIUM (interest rate, occupancy)

**Best For:**
- Building rental portfolio
- Long-term wealth strategy
- Strong rental market
- Tax benefits important
- You purchased with 20%+ down

**Typical ROI:** 8-15% cash-on-cash annually + appreciation

**‚ö†Ô∏è Note:** Doesn't work well with 3-5% down (insufficient equity to pull cash out)

---

### Strategy 4: HYBRID (Mix of Strategies)

**What It Is:**
Combination approach - convert and sell some units, keep others as rentals, or phase conversion over time.

**Process:**
1. Identify best units for conversion
2. Convert/sell those units first
3. Keep remaining as rentals
4. Optionally convert more later

**Financial Profile:**
- **Profit Potential:** MEDIUM-HIGH (blended)
- **Timeline:** MEDIUM (6-15 months)
- **Complexity:** HIGH (managing multiple strategies)
- **Risk:** MEDIUM (diversified risk)

**Best For:**
- Market uncertainty
- Want optionality
- Test conversion demand
- Balanced risk/reward
- Phased execution preferred

**Examples:**
- 4-plex: Convert 2 to condos, rent 2
- 6-plex: Sell 4 individually, keep 2 for income
- Triplex: Sell 2, house hack 1

**Typical ROI:** 300-800% (blended based on mix)

---

### Strategy 5: WHOLESALE (Assign Contract)

**What It Is:**
Get property under contract at below-market price, assign contract to end buyer for a fee, never take ownership.

**Process:**
1. Negotiate purchase (7-14 days)
2. Market to investor buyers
3. Assign contract (+$10K-$50K fee)
4. Close with end buyer (30 days)

**Financial Profile:**
- **Profit Potential:** LOWEST (fixed fee)
- **Timeline:** FASTEST (30-60 days total)
- **Complexity:** LOWEST (no ownership)
- **Risk:** LOWEST (minimal capital at risk)

**Best For:**
- Found great deal, lack capital
- Quick profit needed
- No renovation capacity
- Building buyer relationships
- Testing market

**Typical Profit:** $10K-$50K (fixed assignment fee)

## üí∞ THE 4 FINANCING SCENARIOS

### Option 1: CONVENTIONAL 3% DOWN

**Details:**
- Down payment: 3%
- Interest rate: ~8%
- Loan term: 30 years
- PMI: ~1% of loan amount
- Points: ~2%

**Best For:**
- Limited capital available
- Maximizing leverage
- Strong credit score (680+)
- Investment property

**Pros:**
- ‚úÖ Minimal cash required
- ‚úÖ Huge ROI multiplier
- ‚úÖ Available for 1-4 units

**Cons:**
- ‚ùå PMI adds cost
- ‚ùå Higher rate than 20% down
- ‚ùå Thin equity cushion
- ‚ùå Won't work for BRRRR

---

### Option 2: FHA 3.5% DOWN (Owner-Occupy)

**Details:**
- Down payment: 3.5%
- Interest rate: ~7% (lower than conventional)
- Loan term: 30 years
- MIP: ~1.05% of loan amount
- Must owner-occupy 1 year

**Best For:**
- House hacking
- First-time buyers
- Limited capital
- 2-4 unit properties only

**Pros:**
- ‚úÖ Lowest down payment
- ‚úÖ Lower interest rate
- ‚úÖ Easier qualification
- ‚úÖ Available up to 4-plex

**Cons:**
- ‚ùå Must live there 1 year
- ‚ùå MIP for life (if <10% down)
- ‚ùå Limits immediate conversion
- ‚ùå Property standards strict

---

### Option 3: CONVENTIONAL 20% DOWN

**Details:**
- Down payment: 20%
- Interest rate: ~7.5%
- Loan term: 30 years
- No PMI
- Points: ~1%

**Best For:**
- BRRRR strategy
- Long-term hold
- You have capital
- Want best rates

**Pros:**
- ‚úÖ No PMI
- ‚úÖ Better interest rate
- ‚úÖ Strong equity position
- ‚úÖ Works for BRRRR
- ‚úÖ More lender options

**Cons:**
- ‚ùå More cash required
- ‚ùå Lower ROI multiplier
- ‚ùå Capital tied up

---

### Option 4: HARD MONEY (Short-Term Bridge)

**Details:**
- Down payment: 25%
- Interest rate: ~12%
- Loan term: 1-2 years (balloon)
- Points: 2-4%
- Interest-only payments

**Best For:**
- Quick close needed
- Credit issues
- Short-term play
- Conversion strategy

**Pros:**
- ‚úÖ Fast approval (days)
- ‚úÖ Less strict underwriting
- ‚úÖ Allows major renovations

**Cons:**
- ‚ùå Expensive (high rate + points)

- ‚ùå More cash needed

## üìù HOW TO USE THIS TEMPLATE

### STEP 1: PROPERTY SETUP (5-10 minutes)

**Go to "Property Setup" sheet:**

1. **Property Information (Rows 4-12)**
   - Enter address
   - Select property type
   - Number of units (2-10)
   - Total building SF
   - Lot size
   - Year built
   - Zoning

2. **Individual Unit Details (Rows 15-25)**
   - Complete rows for active units only
   - Leave blank rows for unused units
   - For each unit enter:
     - Bed/bath configuration
     - Square footage
     - Current rent (if occupied)
     - Market rent potential
     - Current condition

**Pro Tips:**
- Use market comps to estimate market rent
- Be conservative on condition assessment
- Verify actual SF (don't trust listing)
- Check zoning for conversion eligibility

---

### STEP 2: ACQUISITION (10-15 minutes)

**Go to "Acquisition" sheet:**

1. **Purchase Details (Rows 4-8)**
   - Purchase price (negotiate!)
   - Closing costs % (2-3% typical)
   - Inspection/due diligence budget

2. **Financing Options (Rows 12-19)**
   - Review 4 pre-populated scenarios
   - Customize interest rates (get actual quotes)
   - Adjust down payment % if needed
   - Update loan terms

3. **Review Calculations (Rows 22-27)**
   - Cash required at closing (each option)
   - Monthly payment (each option)
   - Compare options side-by-side
   - **Choose best fit for your strategy**

**Decision Framework:**
- **3% down:** If maximizing leverage, short hold
- **3.5% FHA:** If house hacking, want lowest rate
- **20% down:** If BRRRR strategy, long-term hold
- **Hard money:** If quick close needed, good deal

---

### STEP 3: RENOVATION BUDGET (15-30 minutes)

**Go to "Renovation Budget" sheet:**

1. **Get Contractor Bids First**
   - Walk property with 2-3 contractors
   - Get itemized bids by unit
   - Use actual numbers (not guesses)

2. **Enter Costs by Unit (Rows 6-18)**
   - **13 categories:**
     - Kitchen updates
     - Bathroom updates
     - Flooring
     - Paint (interior/exterior)
     - Appliances
     - Plumbing
     - Electrical
     - HVAC
     - Windows/doors
     - Landscaping
     - Roof repairs
     - Other
   - Enter costs for units being renovated
   - Leave blank for units not being touched

3. **Review Totals (Rows 19-22)**
   - Subtotal per unit
   - 10% contingency (automatic)
   - Total renovation cost
   - Cost per unit average

**Renovation Strategies:**

**Strategy A: Full Renovation (All Units)**
- Maximize sale price
- Uniform condition
- Highest cost
- Best for conversion

**Strategy B: Selective Renovation**
- Focus on worst units
- Bring to market standard
- Medium cost
- Good for as-is sale

**Strategy C: Cosmetic Only**
- Paint, clean, stage
- Minimal investment
- Lowest cost
- Good for wholesale

**Strategy D: No Renovation**
- Sell truly as-is
- Zero renovation cost
- Fastest timeline
- Lowest sale price

**Pro Tips:**
- Always add 10-20% contingency
- Use highest contractor bid (not average)
- Phase renovations if capital-constrained
- Consider ROI per unit

### STEP 5: EXIT STRATEGIES (20-30 minutes)

**Go to "Exit Strategies" sheet:**

This is the **most important analysis** - compare all 5 exit strategies to find the best one for your situation.

1. **Strategy 1: Subdivide/Convert (Rows 4-13)**
   
   **Enter:**
   - Individual unit sale prices
   - Get comps for similar condos/townhomes
   - Use $/SF from recent sales
   - Add 10-20% premium for new conversion
   
   **Formula calculates:**
   - Gross proceeds (all units)
   - Selling costs (6% commission + 1.5% closing)
   - Conversion costs (from sheet 4)
   - Net proceeds
   - Total project cost
   - Net profit
   - ROI
   
   **Target ROI:** 500-1,500%

2. **Strategy 2: Sell As-Is (Rows 4-13)**
   
   **Enter:**
   - Investor sale price (cap rate basis)
   - Pull duplex/triplex/quad comps
   - Use price per unit or cap rate method
   
   **Cap Rate Method:**
   ```
   Annual NOI √∑ Cap Rate = Property Value
   
   Example:
   Annual NOI: $40,000
   Market Cap Rate: 6.5%
   Value: $40,000 √∑ 0.065 = $615,385
   ```
   
   **Target ROI:** 200-400%

3. **Strategy 3: BRRRR (Rows 4-13)**
   
   **Enter:**
   - Stabilized property value (at 95% occupancy)
   - Refinance amount (75% LTV typical)
   - Cash-out proceeds
   - Annual cash flow
   
   **BRRRR Works If:**
   - Purchased with 20%+ down
   - Added significant value via renovation
   - Stabilized at market rents
   - New appraised value 125%+ of purchase
   
   **Target ROI:** 8-15% cash-on-cash + appreciation

4. **Strategy 4: Hybrid (Rows 4-13)**
   
   **Enter:**
   - Number of units to convert
   - Number of units to keep
   - Sale prices (converted units)
   - Rental value (kept units)
   
   **Example Scenarios:**
   - 4-plex: Sell 2 condos, rent 2 units
   - 6-plex: Sell 4 condos, rent 2 units
   - Triplex: Sell 2, house hack 1
   
   **Target ROI:** 300-800% (blended)

5. **Strategy 5: Wholesale (Rows 4-13)**
   
   **Enter:**
   - End buyer price
   - Your assignment fee ($10K-$50K typical)
   - Minimal holding costs
   
   **Wholesale Formula:**
   ```
   ARV √ó 70% - Repairs - Fee = Max Offer
   
   Example:
   ARV: $800,000
   Repairs: $100,000
   Your Fee: $25,000
   Max Offer: ($800K √ó 0.70) - $100K - $25K = $435K
   ```
   
   **Target Profit:** $10K-$50K fixed fee

6. **Compare All 5 (Row 14)**
   - Side-by-side comparison
   - ROI ranking
   - Timeline comparison
   - Risk assessment

**Decision Matrix:**

| Situation | Best Strategy |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Max profit, have time** | Strategy 1 (Subdivide) |
| **Quick exit, simple** | Strategy 2 (Sell As-Is) |
| **Building portfolio** | Strategy 3 (BRRRR) |
| **Want optionality** | Strategy 4 (Hybrid) |
| **No capital, found deal** | Strategy 5 (Wholesale) |

---

### STEP 6: CASH FLOW (5-10 minutes)

**Go to "Cash Flow" sheet:**

1. **Review 18-Month Projection**
   - Rental income (by month)
   - Loan payments
   - Operating expenses
   - Renovation costs (timed)
   - Conversion costs (timed)
   - Net monthly cash flow
   - Cumulative cash position

2. **Identify Capital Needs**
   - Months with negative cash flow
   - Maximum negative cumulative
   - Total capital required
   - When you'll be cash-positive

3. **Plan Financing**
   - Bridge negative months
   - Line of credit?
   - Partner capital?
   - Staged renovations?

**Cash Flow Patterns:**

**Months 1-3: Acquisition Phase**
- Down payment outflow
- Closing costs
- Start receiving rent (if occupied)
- Loan payments begin

**Months 4-6: Heavy Renovation**
- Large cash outflows
- May have vacancy (no rent)
- Maximum negative cash flow period
- **Need reserves here**

**Months 7-12: Conversion Process**
- Legal costs spread out
- Units coming back online
- Rent ramping up
- Approaching breakeven

**Months 13-18: Sales Phase**
- Marketing costs
- Units listing
- Sales closing
- Cash coming in

## üéì CONVERSION STRATEGIES EXPLAINED

### CONDO CONVERSION

**What It Is:**
Transform a multi-unit property into a condominium where each unit can be individually owned and sold.

**Legal Process:**
1. File Declaration of Condominium with county
2. Create Condominium Association (HOA)
3. Record individual unit deeds
4. Establish CC&Rs (Covenants, Conditions & Restrictions)
5. Separate utilities (if not already)
6. Get county approval

**Timeline:** 6-12 months

**Cost Range:**
- Duplex: $15K-$30K
- Triplex: $20K-$40K
- Quadplex: $25K-$50K
- 5-10 units: $50K-$100K+

**Best For:**
- Urban/suburban markets
- Strong owner-occupant demand
- Units similar in size/quality
- Good location
- Modern construction

**Pricing:**
Individual condos sell at 15-30% premium vs. property as-is

---

### TOWNHOME SUBDIVISION

**What It Is:**
Physically subdivide the lot into separate parcels, creating individual townhome properties with their own land.

**Legal Process:**
1. Survey property for lot line adjustment
2. File subdivision plat with county
3. Create separate legal descriptions
4. Establish shared area agreements (if needed)
5. Separate utilities
6. Get county approval

**Timeline:** 9-18 months (more complex)

**Cost Range:**
- Generally 50% higher than condo conversion
- Requires more extensive surveying
- May need infrastructure improvements

**Best For:**
- Suburban/rural areas
- Properties with adequate land
- Row houses or side-by-side units
- Buyers wanting land ownership
- Areas where condos stigmatized

**Pricing:**
Townhomes sell at 20-40% premium vs. multi-unit as-is

---

### CO-OP CONVERSION

**What It Is:**
Create a cooperative corporation where buyers purchase shares in the corporation, not the physical unit itself.

**Legal Process:**
1. Form cooperative corporation
2. Create proprietary leases for each unit
3. Establish board of directors
4. File with county
5. Issue shares
6. Sell shares (not real estate)

**Timeline:** 4-8 months (fastest)

**Cost Range:**
- Generally 40% less than condo conversion
- Simpler legal process
- Less common (harder to sell)

**Best For:**
- NYC and select urban markets
- Buildings with high-end units
- Sophisticated buyer pool
- Tax advantages for corporation

**Pricing:**
Co-ops sell at 10-20% premium vs. multi-unit (lower than condos)

---

### LEGAL SUBDIVISION (LOT SPLIT)

**What It Is:**
Physically divide the parcel into separate lots, each with its own building/unit.

**Legal Process:**
1. Survey and create subdivision plat
2. File lot split application
3. Ensure separate access for each lot
4. Separate utilities and infrastructure
5. Create new APNs (parcel numbers)
6. Get county approval

**Timeline:** 12-24 months (longest)

**Cost Range:**
- Most expensive option
- May require infrastructure improvements
- Street access, utilities, etc.
- $50K-$200K+ depending on scope

**Best For:**
- Large lots (can be physically divided)
- Detached units (not connected)
- Rural/suburban areas
- Buyers wanting maximum independence

**Pricing:**
Separate lots sell at 30-50% premium vs. multi-unit

## üö® COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

### 1. Not Checking Zoning BEFORE Buying

**Mistake:**
Buy property assuming condo conversion is allowed, find out after closing it's prohibited.

**Solution:**
- Call county planning department FIRST
- Ask: "Is condo conversion allowed at [address]?"
- Get answer in writing
- Make offer contingent on conversion approval

**Red Flags:**
- "Zoning doesn't permit"
- "HOA prohibits subdivisions"
- "Moratorium on conversions"
- "Previous conversion denied"

---

### 2. Underestimating Conversion Timeline

**Mistake:**
Assume 3-month conversion process, actually takes 12 months, blowing up your pro forma.

**Solution:**
- Add 3-6 months buffer to estimates
- Plan for worst-case timeline
- Have capital reserves for extended hold
- Consider selling as-is if delayed

**Reality:**
- County says: "6-9 months" ‚Üí Plan for 12 months
- Attorney says: "4 months" ‚Üí Plan for 6 months
- Always double timeline estimates

---

### 3. Not Getting Actual Contractor Bids

**Mistake:**
Estimate renovation at $15K per unit, reality is $30K per unit.

**Solution:**
- Walk property with 3 contractors
- Get detailed bids (not ballpark)
- Use HIGHEST bid (not average)
- Add 15-20% contingency
- Budget for unknowns (walls, foundation, etc.)

**Conservative Budgeting:**
```

Contractor B bid: $28,000
Contractor C bid: $32,000

Use: $32,000 (highest)
Add contingency: $32,000 √ó 1.15 = $36,800
Budget: $36,800 per unit
```

**Mistake:**

- **Weakening:** 6+ months inventory, price cuts
- **Weak:** 12+ months inventory, falling prices
